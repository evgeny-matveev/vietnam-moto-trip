# Đà Nẵng–Đà Lạt quiet-roads scooter loop

**12 days · roughly 1,850–2,050 km · 150 cc scooters · up to six riders**

This is a road trip first: coast, forest, high passes, small towns and enough empty time for an unexpected swim, market, coffee farm or village road. The outward line runs south and east through Măng Đen and the coast; the return runs west through Lắk, Pleiku, Kon Tum and the Hồ Chí Minh Road. Apart from unavoidable city approaches, it does not repeat the same road.

The route avoids long stretches of QL1A. It uses QL14 only where the late northbound highland link makes it useful, and the final days may be faster and less secluded. Distances are planning ranges rather than promises: mountain road works, weather and small detours can change a day.

## Day 1 — Đà Nẵng to Tam Thanh

**Road:** Đà Nẵng → Non Nước → Cửa Đại → Võ Chí Công coastal road → Tam Thanh
**Distance:** 95–115 km · **Riding:** 3–4 hours

Start with the sea on the left and avoid the direct QL1A run. Cross the rice fields around Hội An rather than entering the old-town traffic, then stay east on the coast. The short opening creates room to wander and makes the K’Bang overnight possible later.

Choose freely:

- breakfast or coffee in the Hội An countryside;
- An Bàng, Cửa Đại or any quiet beach lane that looks promising;
- the painted lanes of Tam Thanh;
- a long sunset and early finish near Tam Kỳ.

**Keep:** the coastal line and the deliberately easy first day.

## Day 2 — Tam Thanh to Măng Đen

**Road:** Tam Thanh → Mỹ Khê → Ba Tơ → QL24 → Vi Ô Lắc Pass → Măng Đen
**Distance:** 235–250 km · **Riding:** 6–7 hours

This is the first long day. It crosses the remaining coast, leaves the hot plain through Ba Tơ and climbs into the Central Highlands. Traffic thins after Ba Tơ; the later sweepers, forest and cloud are the reason to accept the distance.

Choose freely:

- a quick sea stop around Mỹ Khê;
- Ba Tơ market and a slow coffee before the climb;
- river pull-offs west of Ba Tơ;
- Vi Ô Lắc viewpoints whenever the clouds open;
- Kon Pring village, Pa Sỹ waterfall or Đăk Ke lake if daylight remains.

## Day 3 — Măng Đen to K’Bang

**Road:** Măng Đen → Hiếu → Trường Sơn Đông / ĐT676 → K’Bang
**Distance:** 195–210 km · **Riding:** 5–6 hours

This is the expedition day: a remote crossing through Hiếu and the Kon Chu Răng landscape. The useful idea from the comparison plan is the Măng Đen–K’Bang line; the realism fix is to sleep in K’Bang rather than force another 110 km to Quy Nhơn. The road-following map puts that old one-day version at roughly 310 km.

Choose freely:

- an early wander through Hiếu village;
- unnamed forest and river pull-offs on Trường Sơn Đông;
- a roadside lunch wherever the remote middle offers one;
- an unmarked side road toward the Kon Chu Răng landscape;
- K’Bang market and an unhurried evening in a small highland town.

The coast can wait. This is exactly the kind of day that gets worse when treated as a mileage target.

## Day 4 — K’Bang to Sông Cầu

**Road:** K’Bang → ĐT669 → An Khê → Quy Nhơn → QL1D → Sông Cầu
**Distance:** 155–180 km · **Riding:** 4–5 hours

Follow ĐT669 through An Khê, descend to the sea and finish on the excellent QL1D cliff road. Sông Cầu is a calmer overnight than central Quy Nhơn and positions the group well for Phú Yên.

Choose freely:

- coffee in An Khê before the descent;
- stop wherever the valley first opens toward the sea;
- a late lunch or seafront pause in Quy Nhơn;
- QL1D cliff pull-offs;
- a fishing cove or beach around Sông Cầu for the night.

## Day 5 — Sông Cầu to Nha Trang

**Road:** Sông Cầu → Phú Yên coast → Tuy Hòa → Vũng Rô → Đại Lãnh → Nha Trang
**Distance:** 210–235 km · **Riding:** 5–7 hours

Weave through the coast rather than settling onto QL1A. Phú Yên offers two strong branches; choosing one keeps the day adventurous without turning it into a race.

Choose one substantial branch:

- **North Phú Yên:** Gành Đá Đĩa and Ô Loan lagoon;
- **South Phú Yên:** Vũng Rô, Bãi Môn and Mũi Điện.

And leave room for a fishing-cove lunch, an unnamed beach south of Tuy Hòa or a last swim at Đại Lãnh.

## Day 6 — Nha Trang to Đà Lạt

**Road:** Nha Trang → Diên Khánh → Khánh Vĩnh → QL27C → Khánh Lê Pass → Đà Lạt
**Distance:** 135–145 km · **Riding:** 4–5 hours

QL27C is one of the journey’s defining roads: tropical heat, a long forest climb, cloud around Hòn Giao and cool highland air by the finish. Do not clutter it with too many attractions.

Choose freely:

- Khánh Vĩnh market before the pass;
- roadside coffee at the base of the climb;
- whichever safe pull-off has the best cloud break;
- quiet lanes around Tuyền Lâm lake after reaching Đà Lạt;
- a neighborhood café instead of the central night-market crush.

## Day 7 — Hike in Đà Lạt

### Primary: Lang Biang trail

Plan roughly **9 km, 650–700 m of ascent and 4–5 hours** for the substantial option. It gives the day a proper change of pace and a clear summit objective. Check the current trail listing and local access before choosing the exact start.

- [Lang Biang Mountain on AllTrails](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/vietnam/lam-d-ng/lang-biang-mountain)
- [Lang Biang highlight on Komoot](https://www.komoot.com/highlight/1111163)

### Shorter alternatives

- **Đa Phú hills:** roughly 4 km, particularly good around dawn or late afternoon. [Đa Phú on AllTrails](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/vietnam/lam-d-ng/da-phu-hills-trail)
- **Thiên Thai / Tuyền Lâm pine trails:** a gentler half-day when the group wants forest rather than a summit.

Leave the second half of the day empty. A bathhouse, café, garden, rainstorm or simply doing nothing is a legitimate outcome.

## Day 8 — Đà Lạt to Lắk Lake

**Road:** Đà Lạt → Tà Nung → ĐT725 → Nam Ban → Đinh Văn → QL27 → Lắk Lake
**Distance:** 180–200 km · **Riding:** 5–6 hours

Head west through coffee country on ĐT725, one of the best additions from the comparison plan. The surface and pace are part of its appeal. This begins the entirely different western return.

Choose freely:

- coffee farms or a small roaster around Tà Nung;
- a silk workshop around Nam Ban if the doors are open;
- Elephant Waterfall as an optional busy stop, not an obligation;
- village lanes between Đinh Văn and Đam Rông;
- the Lắk Lake shore for the last light.

## Day 9 — Lắk Lake to Pleiku via Buôn Ma Thuột

**Road:** Lắk Lake → Buôn Ma Thuột → Buôn Hồ → QL14 → Chư Sê → Pleiku
**Distance:** 230–250 km · **Riding:** 6–7 hours

This purposeful late-trip transfer preserves the slower K’Bang and Phú Yên days. It is the second long day and the point where faster QL14 riding becomes an acceptable trade.

Choose freely:

- early lake light before departure;
- one serious coffee stop in Buôn Ma Thuột;
- Buôn Hồ market or a plantation side lane;
- pepper country around Chư Sê;
- a late dinner after arriving in Pleiku.

Skip the waterfall detour on this version. The compressed variants below create a separate Buôn Ma Thuột overnight when waterfalls or Yok Đôn matter more than the K’Bang split.

## Day 10 — Pleiku to Kon Tum

**Road:** Pleiku → Biển Hồ → tea hills → Tân Sơn → Chư Đăng Ya → Kon Tum
**Distance:** 90–120 km · **Riding:** 3–4 hours

Keep this day short. It exists to reward curiosity: irrigation lanes, tea hills, a volcanic crater and villages rather than mileage.

Choose freely:

- Biển Hồ before day-trippers arrive;
- tea hills and small lanes north of Pleiku;
- walk partway up the Chư Đăng Ya crater;
- stop for lunch wherever a village road feels inviting;
- in Kon Tum, choose the wooden cathedral, Kon Klor bridge or sunset in Kon Kơ Tu village.

## Day 11 — Kon Tum to Khâm Đức

**Road:** Kon Tum → Đắk Tô → Ngọc Hồi → Đắk Glei → Lò Xo Pass → Khâm Đức
**Distance:** 175–195 km · **Riding:** 5–6 hours

Rejoin the Hồ Chí Minh Road through frontier country. It is more purposeful than the previous day but still scenic, with river valleys and the long Lò Xo crossing.

Choose freely:

- the Đắk Tô historical landscape;
- Ngọc Hồi market;
- an out-and-back toward Bờ Y if frontier geography interests the group;
- quiet stops beside the Đắk Pô Kô river;
- Lò Xo viewpoints and waterfall pull-offs when the weather allows.

## Day 12 — Khâm Đức to Đà Nẵng

**Road:** Khâm Đức → Hồ Chí Minh Road → Thạnh Mỹ → Vũ Gia valley lanes → Hòa Khương → Đà Nẵng
**Distance:** 140–165 km · **Riding:** 4–5 hours

Finish through the mountains and the Vũ Gia valley. This is the day when faster roads are acceptable if time has slipped, but the village lanes beside the river make a better ending.

Choose freely:

- Grăng waterfall as one last detour;
- a slow breakfast or market stop in Thạnh Mỹ;
- whichever Vũ Gia village lane keeps you beside the river;
- an obvious local swimming place;
- a ceremonial finish at the Đà Nẵng seafront.

## Route variants

The app draws each variant as a complete loop. They are substitutions, not extra attractions to stack onto the main plan. The three variants below use the more demanding compressed southbound schedule: Quảng Ngãi on Day 1, Măng Đen on Day 2, the roughly 310 km Măng Đen–K’Bang–Quy Nhơn crossing on Day 3, and Nha Trang on Day 4.

### Ninh Thuận: the most epic road variant

This is the strongest alternative when road variety matters more than easy pacing.

- **Day 5:** Nha Trang → Cam Ranh → Bình Lập → Bình Tiên → Vĩnh Hy → Núi Chúa coast → Phan Rang, 140–170 km.
- **Day 6:** Phan Rang → Po Klong Garai → QL27 → Sông Pha → Ngoạn Mục Pass → Đà Lạt, 110–135 km.
- **Day 7:** Đà Lạt hiking day.
- **Day 8:** Đà Lạt → Lắk Lake.
- **Day 9:** Lắk Lake → Buôn Ma Thuột → Pleiku, 230–250 km.
- **Days 10–12:** Pleiku → Kon Tum → Khâm Đức → Đà Nẵng as in the main loop.

The reward is the dry Núi Chúa coast and Ngoạn Mục, a completely different pass from Khánh Lê. The cost is real: Days 3 and 9 are both long. See the [official Ninh Thuận overview](https://www.vietnam.travel/things-to-do/ninh-thuan-beyond-sun-wind-and-beach).

### Bidoup–Núi Bà: the deeper hiking variant

This also uses the compressed first five days, then replaces the single hiking day with a two-day official trek:

- **Days 6–7:** guided Bidoup–Núi Bà forest route;
- **Day 8:** Đà Lạt → Lắk Lake;
- **Day 9:** Lắk Lake → Buôn Ma Thuột → Pleiku, 230–250 km;
- **Days 10–12:** the main route from Pleiku north.

Choose it when two days in montane forest matter more than the K’Bang overnight. Use the [official Bidoup–Núi Bà route information](https://bidoupnuiba.gov.vn/tuyen-langbiang-1-ngay-2/) rather than inventing an unguided track.

### Yok Đôn: the forest and wildlife variant

This uses the compressed southbound line and keeps a Buôn Ma Thuột overnight. Replace its Day 8 with **Lắk Lake → Yok Đôn → Buôn Ma Thuột**, about 185–220 km plus park time. Skip Dray Sáp, Dray Nur and the museum that day. Choose a current activity from the [official Yok Đôn tours](https://tour.yokdonnationalpark.vn/EN/tours).

## Where spontaneity fits best

- **Day 1:** pick a beach lane rather than a named attraction.
- **Day 3:** take time at an unnamed forest or river stop; K’Bang now prevents that choice from making the day impossible.
- **Day 4:** pause in Quy Nhơn or continue early to a Sông Cầu cove.
- **Day 5:** decide the Phú Yên branch over breakfast according to weather and energy.
- **Day 7:** choose the hike only after seeing the cloud on the mountains.
- **Day 8:** follow one promising coffee-country side road and accept that another stop may disappear.
- **Day 10:** the short distance leaves space for Chư Đăng Ya, a village lunch or an early Kon Tum arrival.
- **Day 12:** choose between the slower Vũ Gia river finish and a direct return according to the group’s mood.

## What from the comparison plan was useful

The useful ideas were incorporated rather than copied wholesale:

- **Măng Đen and K’Bang** create a remote interior crossing instead of an early return to the highway. K’Bang is now an overnight because actual road geometry showed the through-day estimate was wrong.
- **ĐT725 west of Đà Lạt** makes the return genuinely different and gives the trip more texture.
- **Pleiku and Kon Tum** deserve a slow short day, not just fuel stops on a northbound transfer.
- **Khâm Đức and the Hồ Chí Minh Road** provide a coherent mountain finish back to Đà Nẵng.
- **Ninh Thuận and Ngoạn Mục Pass** remain a clearly explained variant because they are excellent but demand a more compressed rhythm.

What was rejected: any line that repeats the outbound road, pushes too much QL1A, or treats all named sights as compulsory. A route can be technically possible and still feel like a checklist; the main plan protects the riding rhythm.

## Open questions that could still improve the route

1. Which matters more to your group: the humane K’Bang split, the harder Núi Chúa–Ngoạn Mục road variant, or the two-day Bidoup forest trek?
2. What month do you expect to ride? Rain, cloud and heat could change which passes and hikes deserve more time.
3. Does the group prefer very early departures and long afternoons off, or slower mornings and riding into the last light?
4. On the coast, are you more drawn to quiet swimming coves, fishing villages, Gành Đá Đĩa or the cape road around Mũi Điện?
5. Are waterfalls around Buôn Ma Thuột important enough to choose a compressed variant instead of the K’Bang overnight?
