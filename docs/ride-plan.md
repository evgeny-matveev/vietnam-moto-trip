# Highlands south, coast home

This is the complete working guide for one relaxed 12-day scooter loop from Đà Nẵng through the Central Highlands to Đà Lạt, down to Nha Trang and home along the coast. It is written as one route, not a comparison between alternatives. The map is for navigation and quick decisions; this guide explains why the road is worth taking, how each day should unfold and what to remove when weather or daylight changes the plan.

## At a glance

**≈1,750 km · avg. 175 km / 5 hr · Đà Lạt mountain hike · Nha Trang rest day**

- **Trip window:** late September to early October 2026
- **Road priority:** attractive provincial, coastal and village roads; busy highways only where the realistic alternatives are worse
- **Long riding days:** Days 2, 4, 9, 10 and 11
- **No intercity riding:** Days 6 and 8
- **Group assumption:** six adults on six loaded small scooters

The route travels south through the highlands while the group is fresh, reaches the sea at Nha Trang and then uses the coast as a more flexible homeward line. It does not pretend that every kilometre is quiet. Day 4 uses QL14 because there is no sensible parallel transfer, and Days 9–11 may need short QL1A connectors when a coastal road is closed, flooded or unfinished. Those compromises are named rather than hidden.

## Conditions for late September 2026

**Last reviewed: 16 July 2026.** This is planning guidance, not the forecast for the actual ride.

The [National Center for Hydro-Meteorological Forecasting seasonal outlook](https://nchmf.gov.vn/kttvsite/vi-VN/1/ban-tin-du-bao-canh-bao-khi-hau-thoi-han-mua-tren-pham-vi-toan-quoc-tu-thang-8-2026-01-2027-post53548.html) expects September rainfall near the long-term average across most of the route, with continued potential for heavy rain. Afternoon showers, low cloud and wet pass surfaces remain realistic in the highlands. Nha Trang and the central coast are entering their wetter September–December period, so sea conditions can cancel Day 8 even when the city remains usable.

Read the [official short-range forecasts and warnings](https://nchmf.gov.vn/kttvsite/) before every riding day and again before a boat booking. Three decisions matter more than a rigid schedule:

1. Do not enter Vi Ô Lắc or Khánh Lê in heavy rain, lightning or dense fog. Wait somewhere safe with services.
2. Remove side trips before removing the daylight margin. Waterfalls, capes and viewpoints are the first things to skip.
3. Treat local closure information as more reliable than the drawn line on ĐT639 and low coastal lanes.

## Day 1 — Đà Nẵng to Tam Thanh

- **Road:** Non Nước → Hội An countryside → Cửa Đại → quiet Quảng Nam coast
- **Distance:** 95–115 km · **Riding:** 3–4 hours
- **Overnight:** Tam Thanh

The first morning should feel like the door opening rather than a test. Leave home after a proper scooter, luggage and tyre check, then let Đà Nẵng thin out around Non Nước. The Marble Mountains rise abruptly from the coastal plain and make the most substantial early stop, but their caves, pagodas and stone stairs deserve time. If the group starts late or the steps are wet, look from below and keep moving; there will be no shortage of temples and viewpoints later in the loop.

There is value in beginning on familiar roads: small packing mistakes, loose straps or awkward group spacing can be corrected close to home, before the route asks for mountain-road concentration.

Beyond Non Nước, the day changes from city edge to the flat, inhabited landscape around Hội An. Rice-field roads, small bridges and village traffic are the point. Park outside the Ancient Town rather than trying to move six loaded scooters through its pedestrian core. Hội An can absorb half a day without effort, so decide in advance whether it is the day’s main visit or simply a long coffee and walk. An Bàng and Cửa Đại are alternative sea pauses, not obligations to add after a complete old-town visit.

South of Cửa Đại, the road begins to establish the rhythm the route is built around: low coastal lanes, fishing settlements and glimpses of open water instead of QL1A. Nothing is technically difficult, which gives the group room to learn its spacing and regrouping habits before the highlands. Arrive at Tam Thanh with daylight left. Walk the mural lanes when the heat softens, reach the beach and let the first night remain easy. The group should finish Day 1 wanting more road, not already recovering from it.

### Road reality, fuel and food

Traffic is busiest leaving Đà Nẵng and around Hội An; the remaining line is easy and well supplied. Start with full tanks but do not over-plan fuel. Eat at home or around Hội An, then carry water for the exposed coast. If rain makes the rice-field lanes muddy or flooded, use the direct surfaced countryside connectors.

### Places worth considering

- **[Marble Mountains](https://vietnam.travel/things-to-do/around-marble-mountains) — 90–150 min, 1 km detour.** Five limestone peaks contain caves, pagodas and high coastal views. Go early and skip the stone steps when they are slick.
- **[Hội An Ancient Town](https://vietnam.travel/places-to-go/central-vietnam/hoi-an) — 90–180 min, 5 km detour.** The preserved trading-port streets reward an unhurried walk; park outside the pedestrian core.
- **[An Bàng Beach](https://vietnam.travel/places-to-go/central-vietnam/hoi-an) — 30–90 min, 2 km detour.** Choose it for a relaxed café-and-sea stop instead of a long Hội An visit.
- **[Cửa Đại coast](https://vietnam.travel/places-to-go/central-vietnam/hoi-an) — 15–45 min, on route.** The estuary is the visual transition from Hội An countryside to the coastal road.
- **[Tam Thanh mural village](https://quangnamtourism.com.vn/lang-bich-hoa-tam-thanh/) — 45–90 min, on route.** Fishing-village lanes form an open-air mural project with the beach immediately beyond.

- **Stop priority:** Choose one substantial stop—Marble Mountains or Hội An—then let Tam Thanh be the evening.
- **Weather decision:** Skip the Marble Mountains’ slick steps and low rice-field lanes. Use the direct surfaced countryside line to Tam Thanh.
- **Arrival plan:** Check in before sunset, unload and walk the mural village or beach without adding another ride.

### Watch before riding

- [The best of Hội An by motorbike](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8v1rb8kPE3k) — Paddy Doyle, English. A rider’s look at Hội An and the surrounding roads before the route turns south.

### Where to stay in Tam Thanh

One night at the end of Day 1. Prices are per person for six adults.

- **Best view / niche — [House of the Rising Sun at Tam Thanh Beach](https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/33101620): $15–22 / 400–570k VND, about 200–240k above regular.** A whole three-bedroom beachfront house for exactly six, with a kitchen, ocean views and sunrise outside. The listing has few reviews and a low recent host response rate; message before relying on it, and remember that Airbnb shows only an approximate position before booking.
- **Most comfortable — [Tam Thanh Beach Resort & Spa](https://www.booking.com/hotel/vn/tam-thanh-beach-resort-amp-spa.en-gb.html): $16–25 / 420–650k VND, about 220–320k above regular.** Three resort rooms for six, with a beach setting, pool, restaurant and spa.
- **Regular baseline — [Tam Thanh Natural Beach Resort](https://www.agoda.com/tam-thanh-natural-beach-resort_2/hotel/tam-ky-quang-nam-vn.html): $8–13 / 200–330k VND.** Three simple beach rooms or bungalows. Comfort is basic, but the sea and mural village are close.

## Day 2 — Tam Thanh to Măng Đen

- **Road:** Tam Thanh → Ba Tơ → QL24 → Vi Ô Lắc Pass → Măng Đen
- **Distance:** 235–250 km · **Riding:** 6–7 hours
- **Overnight:** Măng Đen

Day 2 is where the journey properly leaves the coast. Start early enough to watch Tam Thanh wake rather than spending the cool hours packing. The first part of the day crosses the warm Quảng Ngãi plain, where the road still feels inhabited and supplies are easy. Ba Tơ is the deliberate hinge: refuel, eat an early lunch and make an honest assessment of the sky before QL24 begins its long climb. Do not leave the last substantial meal or fuel stop until the pass.

The attraction after Ba Tơ is not a sequence of monuments but the steady change in altitude. Heat and broad lowland views give way to forest, tighter bends and the possibility of cloud. Vi Ô Lắc is the first mountain road that asks the group to behave like a group: allow gaps, never overtake for the sake of staying together and regroup only at places fully separated from traffic. A patch of clear road is not permission to rush. The long day is won through rhythm and early decisions, not speed.

This first major climb will also reveal whether the planned daily pace is realistic for the scooters and riders; use that knowledge to make later long days calmer, not faster.

Măng Đen arrives as a genuine contrast to the morning—cooler air, pine-framed streets and a slower town atmosphere. Pa Sỹ Waterfall, Đăk Ke Lake and Kon Pring village are not three boxes to tick after 240 km. If the group reaches town early and everyone still has energy, choose one. Pa Sỹ is the fuller excursion, Đăk Ke the easiest decompression, and Kon Pring the cultural visit that requires the most respectful attention. If arrival is late, go directly to the stay, eat and let the town itself be the reward.

### Road reality, fuel and food

This is the route’s first remote and daylight-sensitive day. Fill in Ba Tơ even if the gauges look comfortable, eat before climbing and carry water and a simple snack. Expect cooler air and reduced visibility at altitude. Use only secure pull-offs, and stop before fatigue turns into poor cornering.

### Places worth considering

- **[Ba Tơ](https://csdl.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/dest/?item=75) — 30–60 min, on route.** A market and historical pause that is also the practical meal-and-fuel stop before the climb.
- **[Vi Ô Lắc Pass](https://csdl.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/dest/?item=75) — 15–30 min, on route.** The defining forested climb. Skip exposed viewpoints in heavy rain or dense cloud.
- **[Pa Sỹ Waterfall](https://nongthon.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/kon-tum-attracts-tourists-with-its-indigenous-culture/?lang=en) — 60–90 min, 8 km detour.** The substantial arrival-day nature choice when daylight remains.
- **[Đăk Ke Lake](https://nongthon.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/kon-tum-attracts-tourists-with-its-indigenous-culture/?lang=en) — 30–60 min, 3 km detour.** A calm pine-framed lake for an easy evening.
- **[Kon Pring village](https://nongthon.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/kon-tum-attracts-tourists-with-its-indigenous-culture/?lang=en) — 45–90 min, 2 km detour.** A Ba Na community centred on its communal house. Visit as guests and ask before photographing people or homes.

- **Stop priority:** Ba Tơ and the pass are fixed; choose no more than one Măng Đen arrival stop.
- **Weather decision:** If Vi Ô Lắc is hidden by heavy rain, lightning or dense cloud, wait in Ba Tơ. Do not begin a late blind crossing.
- **Arrival plan:** Go directly to the booked stay when daylight is short; Pa Sỹ, Đăk Ke and Kon Pring are optional.

### Watch before riding

- [Four days in Măng Đen](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T-os-TucW6M) — Life Number 6, English. A feel for the pine-town atmosphere waiting after the climb.
- [Food and highland life in Kon Tum](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9YjhYj2g0gI) — Khoai Lang Thang, Vietnamese. Cultural and food context for Days 2 and 3.

### Where to stay in Măng Đen

Reserve one night after the Vi Ô Lắc ride.

- **Best view / niche — [De Vivre Homestay Măng Đen](https://www.booking.com/hotel/vn/khach-san-de-vivre.html): $12–17 / 300–430k VND, about 60–80k above regular.** A six-person family room with three large doubles; tents can be requested. Confirm that any tent has proper rain cover and bathroom access.
- **Most comfortable — [Diamond Luxury Hotel & Cafe Măng Đen](https://www.booking.com/hotel/vn/diamond-luxury-amp-cafe-mang-den.html): $14–20 / 370–530k VND, about 150–160k above regular.** One newer deluxe family room with three double beds, avoiding a room split.
- **Regular baseline — [Bình Sơn Măng Đen Villa](https://www.booking.com/hotel/vn/binh-son-mang-den-villa-mang-den.en-gb.html): $8–14 / 220–370k VND.** A quieter villa-style six-person setup. Confirm the selected unit really includes all three advertised beds.

## Day 3 — Măng Đen to Kon Tum to Pleiku

- **Road:** Măng Đen → Kon Tum → Biển Hồ lanes → Pleiku
- **Distance:** 100–125 km · **Riding:** 3–4 hours
- **Overnight:** Pleiku

After Day 2, the short third day is an invitation to look more closely. Leave Măng Đen without racing the morning. The descent toward Kon Tum gradually trades pine and cool air for a broader river valley. Because the distance is modest, Kon Tum can be treated as a place rather than a fuel stop. The Wooden Church, Kon Klor communal house and suspension bridge form a coherent introduction to the city’s meeting of Catholic, colonial and Ba Na architectural traditions.

Kon Kơ Tu extends that story beyond formal landmarks into a living Ba Na community. It deserves slower behaviour: ask before entering spaces or photographing people, keep the scooters unobtrusive and do not turn homes into exhibits. If the group wants more time for Pleiku’s landscape, leave the village detour for another trip and keep the church–Kon Klor pairing as the day’s cultural core.

The road toward Pleiku opens into tea and coffee country. Near the city, Biển Hồ is the easier decision: an ancient volcanic crater lake among pine, close enough to visit without compromising the evening. Chư Đăng Ya asks for more—extra distance, exposed slopes and dry conditions—but repays that effort with a landscape where cultivation makes the volcanic form visible. Choose it only when the afternoon remains bright and the group is unhurried. Pleiku is the practical overnight city, yet the day should finish outside the traffic if possible: at Biển Hồ, among the tea lanes or at the farmstay rather than circling urban sights.

The contrast is what makes the day memorable: communal roofs and river culture in Kon Tum give way to open agricultural highlands and the unmistakable volcanic geography around Pleiku.

### Road reality, fuel and food

The riding is moderate and services are frequent in Kon Tum and Pleiku. Refuel before leaving Kon Tum if taking rural lanes around Biển Hồ or Chư Đăng Ya. Use the spare time for a proper lunch rather than collecting stops. Unsealed approaches can become slick after rain.

### Places worth considering

- **[Kon Tum Wooden Church](https://nongthon.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/kon-tum-attracts-tourists-with-its-indigenous-culture/?lang=en) — 30–60 min, 1 km detour.** A century-old cathedral combining Roman forms, local materials and Ba Na stilt-house influence.
- **[Kon Klor communal house and bridge](https://nongthon.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/kon-tum-attracts-tourists-with-its-indigenous-culture/?lang=en) — 30–60 min, 3 km detour.** A monumental communal roof beside the long bridge over the Đăk Bla River.
- **[Kon Kơ Tu village](https://nongthon.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/kon-tum-attracts-tourists-with-its-indigenous-culture/?lang=en) — 45–90 min, 7 km detour.** A living Ba Na village; behave as visitors, not spectators.
- **[Biển Hồ](https://vietnam.travel/node/1865) — 30–60 min, 7 km detour.** Pleiku’s pine-framed volcanic crater lake.
- **[Chư Đăng Ya volcano](https://vietnam.travel/node/1865) — 60–90 min, 15 km detour.** A farmed extinct crater for a dry, unhurried afternoon only.

- **Stop priority:** Give Kon Tum one coherent cultural visit, then choose Biển Hồ or Chư Đăng Ya rather than both.
- **Weather decision:** Skip Chư Đăng Ya’s exposed, potentially muddy slopes and ride directly to Pleiku.
- **Arrival plan:** Reach the farmstay or city hotel before dusk and keep the evening free for food and recovery.

### Watch before riding

- [Pleiku and Kon Tum: Central Highlands](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XhrTnyDLipk) — My Layover Life, English. A compact overview of both cities and the landscapes between them.

### Where to stay in Pleiku

The farmstay is the unusually strong value choice here.

- **Best view / niche — [An Nhiên Farmstay & Resort — Full House](https://www.booking.com/hotel/vn/xom-organic-farm-stay.en-gb.html): $5–8 / 130–200k VND, saving about 100–170k against regular.** A full house for six among vegetable, rice and coffee fields, with a garden and barbecue area.
- **Most comfortable — [Mường Thanh Grand Gia Lai](https://www.booking.com/hotel/vn/muong-thanh-grand-gia-lai.en-gb.html): $20–30 / 530–780k VND, about 300–410k above regular.** Three rooms with full hotel service, pool, spa and restaurant.
- **Regular baseline — [Adalie Pleiku Hotel](https://www.booking.com/hotel/vn/adalie-pleiku.html): $9–14 / 230–370k VND.** One family room plus one double or twin: clean, practical and sensibly located.

## Day 4 — Pleiku to Buôn Ma Thuột to Lắk Lake

- **Road:** Pleiku → Chư Sê → Buôn Hồ → Buôn Ma Thuột bypass → Lắk Lake
- **Distance:** 225–245 km · **Riding:** 6–7 hours
- **Overnight:** Lắk Lake

Day 4 is the route’s honest compromise. QL14 carries the traffic that the rest of the itinerary tries to avoid, but there is no quiet parallel road that moves the group this far south without turning one demanding transfer into two. Leave Pleiku early, accept that the morning is about steady progress and resist the temptation to recover time by riding close to trucks. The landscape remains highland—plantations, red soil and long settlement strips—but the road experience is more functional than intimate.

Chư Sê, Buôn Hồ and the outskirts of Buôn Ma Thuột divide the transfer into useful checkpoints. Take fuel and food when the opportunity is simple rather than waiting for a perfect stop. Plantation side roads may offer a break from the highway, but use them only when their surface and reconnection are obvious. A quiet-looking lane that adds an hour or dissolves into mud is not an improvement on a disclosed highway section.

Dray Sáp and Dray Nur are major substitutions west of the direct line, not quick roadside waterfalls. Either can become the day’s scenic centre, but choosing one means dropping city wandering, plantation detours and any ambition to reach Lắk early. Do not attempt both casually. The fixed reward is Lắk Lake: a broad sheet of water backed by hills and M’nông villages, most convincing when traffic noise has disappeared and the light is low. If possible, arrive with enough time to leave the scooters parked and walk the shore. The special stay at the tented camp is expensive relative to the route, but this is the night where paying for the setting changes the whole experience.

### Road reality, fuel and food

Expect buses, trucks, road noise and reduced passing space. Keep a generous following distance and never sit beside a heavy vehicle. Start full, refuel around Buôn Hồ or Buôn Ma Thuột and eat before the final Lắk section. Thunderstorms can remove visibility quickly; Buôn Ma Thuột is the sensible stop if the day collapses.

### Places worth considering

- **[Dray Sáp Waterfall](https://images.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/vn/dmdocuments/2022/220925_BCTT_QHHTDL.pdf) — 60–90 min, 25 km detour.** A quieter basalt gorge; choose it instead of city stops.
- **[Dray Nur Waterfall](https://images.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/vn/dmdocuments/2022/220925_BCTT_QHHTDL.pdf) — 60–90 min, 35 km detour.** A wide waterfall curtain over the Sêrêpốk’s basalt edge. It is too far to combine casually with Dray Sáp.
- **[Lắk Lake](https://images.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/vn/dmdocuments/2022/220925_BCTT_QHHTDL.pdf) — 60–120 min, on route.** The day’s fixed destination, best at first or last light.

- **Stop priority:** Lắk Lake is fixed. Choose at most one waterfall and let it replace every other detour.
- **Weather decision:** Remove every waterfall and plantation detour. Continue on the direct surfaced line, or stop in Buôn Ma Thuột if thunderstorms destroy visibility.
- **Arrival plan:** Reach the lake in daylight, check in and leave the scooters parked for the shore at last light.

### Watch before riding

- [Lắk Lake and Buôn Ma Thuột motorbike vlog](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nDq9GMs1uAw) — TIM and FIN, English. It shows the transfer-day rhythm and why the lake is a worthwhile finish.

### Where to stay at Lắk Lake

This is the strongest point on the route to choose experience over price.

- **Best view / niche — [LAK Tented Camp — Lakeview Tents](https://www.booking.com/hotel/vn/lak-tented-camp.en-gb.html): $23–45 / 600k–1.18m VND, about 450–950k above regular.** Two ensuite lakeview tents for six, with boat arrival and lake-and-mountain views. Meal, boat and cultural packages can raise the lower price.
- **Most comfortable — [LAK Tented Camp — Lakefront Bungalows](https://www.booking.com/hotel/vn/lak-tented-camp.en-gb.html): $43–63 / 1.12–1.67m VND, about 970k–1.44m above regular.** Two air-conditioned lakefront bungalows; polished eco-lodge comfort rather than a conventional pool resort.
- **Regular baseline — [Lak View Hotel](https://www.booking.com/hotel/vn/lak-view.en-gb.html): $6–9 / 150–230k VND.** Three basic double rooms near the lake when the camp is unavailable or too expensive.

## Day 5 — Lắk Lake to Đà Lạt

- **Road:** QL27 → Đinh Văn area → Nam Ban → ĐT725 → Tà Nung → Đà Lạt
- **Distance:** 180–200 km · **Riding:** 5–6 hours
- **Overnight:** Đà Lạt, first of two nights

Give Lắk Lake one quiet hour before turning toward Đà Lạt. Morning water and hills are part of the reason for staying here; leaving in darkness simply converts a special overnight into another bed. Once moving, the day follows a western highland line through cultivated country rather than taking the fastest transfer. Villages, coffee land and the gradual approach to the Đà Lạt plateau supply the interest, but they also keep speeds modest and make surface changes after rain more important.

The road should feel measured. Take a real coffee stop where the group can get off the scooters and notice the crop landscape instead of treating coffee country as scenery passing behind visors. Elephant Waterfall sits naturally beside the Nam Ban approach, but access to the lower path can change and wet steps can be poor value after a long morning. It is an optional stop only when local access is confirmed, the group is ahead of time and the surface is safe.

The final Tà Nung approach is the part to protect. The climb gathers the scattered farms and roasters into the cooler, denser atmosphere of Đà Lạt. Traffic increases near the city, but the arrival is more satisfying for having come through the western countryside. Check into the same accommodation for two nights, unload completely and stop thinking about scooters. Tuyền Lâm can wait for the hiking day; the best first evening is simply food, a short city walk and an early decision about tomorrow’s weather-dependent plan.

Because the next morning has no intercity departure, there is no reward for entering Đà Lạt exhausted. Preserve enough attention for the busier final kilometres and a calm two-night check-in.

### Road reality, fuel and food

Surfaces and drainage can vary after rain, especially on farm connectors. Begin with enough fuel for a relaxed departure, then top up in the Đinh Văn or Nam Ban area before smaller western roads. Eat before the Tà Nung climb and keep the afternoon margin for urban traffic and check-in.

### Places worth considering

- **[Lắk Lake](https://images.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/vn/dmdocuments/2022/220925_BCTT_QHHTDL.pdf) — 60–120 min, on route.** Use the morning rather than leaving the lake unseen.

## Day 6 — Đà Lạt mountain day

- **Road:** No intercity riding
- **Distance:** 4–10 km hiking, depending on the chosen plan
- **Overnight:** Đà Lạt, second night of the same booking

Wake without luggage or a departure deadline. Day 6 exists to exchange the narrow view from a saddle for a slower understanding of the plateau. Do not choose the hike from home weeks in advance; choose it after looking at the ridges, checking the rain and asking how the group’s legs feel after five days. Clear summits, wet forest and a tired group produce different good days.

Lang Biang is the serious mountain objective: roughly 9.7 km out and back, about 685 m of ascent and 4–4.5 hours for the normal route. The steep final climb and rocky ground make it inappropriate in rain. Start early, carry water and a rain layer, pay the required entry and keep the rest of the day empty. Đa Phú is the shorter open-ridge answer, around 4 km with 195 m of ascent. Its opening can be steep and rocky, while the exposed ridge gives little shelter from sun or a sudden storm.

If the high ground is hidden, use the day rather than mourning it. Xuân Hương Lake offers a paved recovery loop of about 7 km. Tuyền Lâm provides pine and water, but only on short signed routes confirmed locally; old race tracks cross private land and are not independent hiking instructions. The official Bidoup–Núi Bà forest programme is a different kind of full day: pre-booked, guided and centred on forest and K’Ho interpretation rather than a summit. It normally occupies roughly 08:00–15:00 including the visitor-area programme.

The day should end with clean clothes, checked scooters and a quiet meal. It is the route’s recovery point as much as its hiking point. Do not turn a mountain morning into an afternoon checklist of city attractions.

### Road reality, fuel and food

Leave the loaded scooters parked. Download the selected trail before leaving town, carry water, food, grip footwear and a light rain layer. For remote or guided options, confirm transport and return time. Budget ordinary meals as usual; entry and minor transport sit inside the routine-fee allowance.

## Day 7 — Đà Lạt to Nha Trang

- **Road:** Đà Lạt → Bidoup–Núi Bà / Hòn Giao → Khánh Lê Pass → Khánh Vĩnh → Nha Trang
- **Distance:** 135–145 km · **Riding:** 4–5 hours
- **Overnight:** Nha Trang, first of two nights

Day 7 is not long by distance, but it contains the route’s most consequential descent. Leave Đà Lạt only after confirming that Khánh Lê is open and visible. The first hours pass through the high forest around Bidoup–Núi Bà and Hòn Giao, where cloud, vegetation and temperature make the plateau feel far removed from the coast waiting below. This is a transit through a protected mountain landscape, not a second trekking day; unsigned forest tracks are not invitations.

At the pass, the road begins shedding altitude for a long time. Treat the descent as its own discipline. Use engine braking where the scooter permits it, leave a wide gap, avoid holding the brakes continuously and stop at a secure pull-off if a machine needs to cool. Never chase a faster rider and never stop six scooters on the edge of a blind bend for a photograph. The attraction is the changing forest and the sensation of moving from cool highland air toward tropical lowlands, not a speed test.

Below Khánh Vĩnh, the road broadens and the day gradually becomes easier. Take a proper pause before the final city approach so tired concentration does not meet Nha Trang traffic. Arrival at the sea should feel dramatic after a week in the highlands. Check into one base for two nights, unload completely and leave the first evening unscheduled. A walk along Nha Trang Beach and an easy dinner are enough. The rest day works because there is no pressure to begin sightseeing tonight.

The change in temperature and humidity can be as striking as the view; give bodies and machines time to settle rather than carrying mountain pace into the lowland traffic.

### Road reality, fuel and food

Start with full tanks and eat before leaving Đà Lạt. Services are sparse on the high section and more reliable after Khánh Vĩnh. Read the [Khánh Hòa Police pass guidance](https://congan.khanhhoa.gov.vn/vi/ky-nang-can-thiet-khi-lai-xe-tren-duong-deo-khanh-le), check brakes before departure and protect a large weather margin.

### Places worth considering

- **[Nha Trang Beach](https://vietnam.travel/places-to-go/central-vietnam/nha-trang) — 60–180 min, on route.** A six-kilometre promenade and beach that makes the arrival feel like a real pause.

- **Stop priority:** The forest and safe descent are the attraction; do not turn park context into an extra trek.
- **Weather decision:** Do not start Khánh Lê in heavy rain or dense fog. Wait in Đà Lạt; if conditions remain poor, arrange vehicle transport rather than forcing the descent.
- **Arrival plan:** Check in for both nights, unload and keep the first Nha Trang evening limited to beach and dinner.

## Day 8 — Full Nha Trang day

- **Road:** Nha Trang base; no intercity riding
- **Distance:** No distance target
- **Overnight:** Nha Trang, second night of the same booking

Day 8 is the time recovered by choosing the relaxed route, and it should feel spacious. Do not turn it into an urban stage race. Check the marine forecast before breakfast, then commit to one complete plan. Good sea conditions lead outward into Nha Trang Bay; poor sea conditions lead inward to the city’s Cham, Buddhist, scientific and bathing traditions. Neither is a lesser version of the day.

With a calm forecast and no warning, choose an established Hòn Mun or island operator with current permits and safety arrangements. The protected marine area and reef habitat make the bay the strongest reason to spend the day on a boat, but a sea plan consumes most of the day. Do not promise yourselves museums and towers afterward. Return, shower, walk the promenade and keep the evening free.

When swell, rain or thunder makes the bay unsuitable, build a city day from three well-chosen stops. Po Nagar gives the deepest historical anchor: a living Cham temple complex above the river. The National Oceanographic Museum supplies a slower indoor block and marine context. Long Sơn, Hòn Chồng and a hot mineral mud bath then offer different endings—spiritual, coastal or restorative. Select one ending rather than squeezing all three together. A full VinWonders day is a third independent plan, not something added around the city or boat itinerary.

The most important achievement is that nobody packs a scooter. The second night in one hotel lets wet clothes dry, batteries charge and the group recover before the coast-home stage.

This pause also separates two distinct journeys: the demanding highland descent is complete, while tomorrow begins the longer, warmer coastal return with different traffic and weather decisions.

### Road reality, fuel and food

Leave loaded scooters parked. City movement needs little fuel; use taxis or short unloaded rides if appropriate. Boat and theme-park plans usually control the meal schedule, while a city day works best with a slow lunch. Recheck warnings before paying a non-refundable operator.

## Day 9 — Nha Trang to Sông Cầu

- **Road:** Nha Trang → Đại Lãnh → Vũng Rô or northern Phú Yên → Tuy Hòa → Sông Cầu
- **Distance:** 210–235 km · **Riding:** 5–7 hours
- **Overnight:** Sông Cầu

The journey turns home on Day 9, but it does not yet become easy. Leave Nha Trang early enough to clear the urban strip before the day warms. The first reward is Đại Lãnh, where the road meets a broad bay beneath the pass. It is an ideal breakfast or coffee pause and the point to confirm which Phú Yên story the day will tell.

The southern branch uses Vũng Rô, Bãi Môn and Mũi Điện. It is a day of mountain-backed water, fishing life, maritime history and a lighthouse above a secluded beach. The capes and stairways require time off the scooters, so choosing this branch means passing through the north more directly. The northern branch saves its attention for Ô Loan Lagoon and Gành Đá Đĩa, where low coastal water and interlocking basalt columns produce a different landscape. Both are substantial; attempting both turns a beautiful day into a daylight calculation.

Tuy Hòa is the practical middle: fuel, food and the place to admit when a branch has taken longer than expected. North of the city, direct progress includes short QL1A gaps because no continuous quiet parallel road exists. Accept those connectors rather than improvising long village detours late in the day. Sông Cầu is the deliberate finish—a quieter bay-and-beach overnight that keeps the group out of central Quy Nhơn and places the QL1D cliff road at the start of tomorrow. Arrive with enough light to see why the coast was chosen.

The day succeeds when one branch is experienced properly and the final beach still has evening light; visiting both headline branches but reaching Sông Cầu exhausted is the wrong trade.

### Road reality, fuel and food

Start full, top up around Tuy Hòa and do not enter remote cape roads on reserve. Breakfast near Đại Lãnh and a proper Tuy Hòa meal fit the day. Cape and reef detours are exposed, and short QL1A sections demand disciplined spacing around trucks.

## Day 10 — Sông Cầu to Quy Nhơn to Sa Huỳnh

- **Road:** QL1D → Quy Nhơn → Cát Tiến → Đề Gi → Lộ Diêu → Sa Huỳnh
- **Distance:** 180–215 km · **Riding:** 5–7 hours
- **Overnight:** Sa Huỳnh

Day 10 begins with one of the easiest stretches to love. QL1D follows the coast toward Quy Nhơn through cliff bends and open sea views; use only pull-offs that fully separate the scooters from traffic. The city then presents too many directions at once. Ghềnh Ráng and Tháp Đôi are the simple pairing, one coastal and one Cham. Bánh Ít draws inland, while Eo Gió draws onto the exposed peninsula. Choose one wider detour at most. A meaningful hour in Quy Nhơn is better than a hurried circuit of landmarks.

North of the city, the preferred line returns to smaller coastal roads through Cát Tiến, Đề Gi and Lộ Diêu. Fishing settlements, long open sections and the sense of travelling beside rather than merely near the sea are the purpose. But this is the route’s provisional day. May 2026 reporting still described land clearance and construction on the Mỹ Thành–Lại Giang section of ĐT639. The named project is not proof that every segment on the drawn route is closed; it is proof that the group must ask locally and respond to the road in front of it.

Do not improvise around barriers or active machinery. If the coast closes, take the shortest confirmed connector to QL1A, accept the unpleasant traffic and return seaward only where the line is known open. The priority is Sa Huỳnh before dark. Its beach, salt landscape and fishing life provide a calm endpoint after a day of choices. Gò Cỏ belongs to this evening only if arrival is early; otherwise it is tomorrow morning’s first place.

Because construction can erase hours of planning, the group should treat every confirmed open coastal kilometre as a bonus and never measure success by perfect adherence to the drawn line.

### Road reality, fuel and food

Fuel in Quy Nhơn before committing to the northern coast and eat there or before leaving the city. ĐT639 status matters more than the GPX. Short QL1A fallbacks bring trucks and noise; use them as transfers, not places to recover time.

## Day 11 — Sa Huỳnh to Phú Ninh Lake

- **Road:** Gò Cỏ → Mỹ Khê → Sơn Mỹ → Bà Làng An → inland Dung Quất bypass → Phú Ninh Lake
- **Distance:** 185–210 km · **Riding:** 5–6 hours
- **Overnight:** Phú Ninh Lake or Tam Kỳ weather fallback

The temptation on Day 11 is to spend the whole morning proving how rich the Quảng Ngãi coast is. Resist it. Start with either Gò Cỏ’s old fishing-village fabric or a longer Sa Huỳnh morning, then move north. Stone walls, salt work and community life make Gò Cỏ more than a scenic lane, but it deserves a real visit; if the previous night ended late, choose a short village walk and preserve the lake.

Mỹ Khê, Sơn Mỹ, Bà Làng An and Thiên Ấn offer very different pauses. Sơn Mỹ is the non-negotiable one when open: a memorial and museum preserving the history of the 1968 Mỹ Lai massacre. Enter quietly, allow the time the site requires and do not frame it as another photo stop. Mỹ Khê is the easy sea break. Bà Làng An adds a volcanic-rock cape and open Lý Sơn view but costs a weather-exposed detour. Thiên Ấn turns inland to a historic pagoda above the Trà Khúc plain. Choose around the group’s energy, never all four.

The route then bends inland around Dung Quất rather than following industrial truck corridors. This is where the day’s purpose changes: the coast is no longer the destination, Phú Ninh is. Protect the final hours. The lake sits close to Tam Kỳ but feels removed from a city overnight, with forested shores, islands and the possibility of quiet water at the end of a long coastal return. Arriving after dark defeats the change. Pre-book the lake stay or approved campsite, reach it while the light remains and let boating or mineral bathing be a bonus only after check-in. If lake access fails, Tam Kỳ seven kilometres away is the dependable fallback.

### Road reality, fuel and food

Start with enough fuel for the coast, then refill around Quảng Ngãi before the inland leg. Eat before leaving the city’s service area. Avoid Dung Quất industrial corridors, low flooded lanes and exposed capes when weather deteriorates. The lake arrival margin is more valuable than one more beach.

### Places worth considering

- **[Sa Huỳnh Beach](https://quangngai.gov.vn/tin-tuc/tin-tu-cac-so-nganh-dia-phuong/du-lich-quang-ngai-sac-xuan-tren-nhung-hanh-trinh-moi.html) — 60–120 min, on route.** Use it only if the previous evening did not already provide the full beach experience.
- **[Gò Cỏ village](https://nongthon.vietnamtourism.gov.vn/explore-go-co-village-heritage-park-a-3-star-ocop-jewel-of-quang-ngai/?lang=en) — 60–120 min, 5 km detour.** Stone walls, salt work and community tourism connect several layers of coastal history.
- **[Mỹ Khê Beach, Quảng Ngãi](https://quangngai.gov.vn/tin-tuc/tin-tu-cac-so-nganh-dia-phuong/du-lich-quang-ngai-sac-xuan-tren-nhung-hanh-trinh-moi.html) — 30–75 min, on route.** A long crescent beach for a simple rest.
- **[Sơn Mỹ Memorial](https://quangngai.gov.vn/tin-tuc/tin-tu-cac-so-nganh-dia-phuong/du-lich-quang-ngai-sac-xuan-tren-nhung-hanh-trinh-moi.html) — 60–90 min, 4 km detour.** A serious memorial and museum. Allow quiet time.

- **[Thiên Ấn Pagoda](https://quangngai.gov.vn/tin-tuc/tin-tu-cac-so-nganh-dia-phuong/du-lich-quang-ngai-sac-xuan-tren-nhung-hanh-trinh-moi.html) — 45–75 min, 7 km detour.** A historic pagoda above the Trà Khúc River and plain.

Confirm accommodation and operations through the [current destination page](https://quangnamtourism.com.vn/ho-phu-ninh/) before the trip.

## Day 12 — Phú Ninh Lake to Đà Nẵng

- **Road:** Phú Ninh Lake → Tam Kỳ countryside → Duy Xuyên → Hội An rice fields → Đà Nẵng
- **Distance:** 95–115 km · **Riding:** 3–4 hours
- **Overnight:** Home; Đà Nẵng hotel is optional

The final morning belongs to the lake. Eat without rushing, look across the water and let tents or wet gear dry enough to pack. There is no mountain deadline and no attraction worth replacing a calm departure. Once moving, use inland Quảng Nam lanes so the route does not simply reverse Day 1. The landscape is inhabited and familiar—fields, village roads, waterways and the long cultural pull of Hội An—but it should now feel like the gradual return home.

Tam Kỳ is the first practical checkpoint for fuel or anything the previous night could not provide. North of town, choose surfaced parallel roads according to the weather. Rice-field lanes are beautiful when dry and frustrating when flooded; the purpose of Day 12 is not to defend a GPX at all costs. Duy Xuyên and the approaches to Hội An offer a final slow section without the exposed coast.

Use Hội An for lunch, not for reopening Day 1’s sightseeing debate. The Ancient Town and Marble Mountains remain on the map because they are useful fallback or revisit points, but the group will likely want to finish. After lunch, ride the known countryside edge toward Đà Nẵng, refuel, return or park the scooters and deal calmly with wet luggage or any small mechanical issue. The easy finish is intentional. The trip ends with time and services around it, not with a final pass crossed to prove something.

That spare margin is part of the route design, not unused time: it safely absorbs a slow breakfast, flooded lanes, a puncture, luggage repacking or simply the desire to be home.

### Road reality, fuel and food

Fuel in Tam Kỳ if needed and plan lunch around Hội An. Traffic increases toward Đà Nẵng, but the day has ample margin. Avoid low rice-field lanes after sustained rain and choose the most direct open surfaced route.

### Places worth considering

- **[Phú Ninh Lake & hot mineral water](https://quangnamtourism.com.vn/en/detailnews/?id=news_2148&t=nine-musttry-experiences-in-quang-nam) — 2–4 hr before departure.** The morning is the final lake experience; do not add paid activities.
- **[Hội An Ancient Town](https://vietnam.travel/places-to-go/central-vietnam/hoi-an) — 90–180 min, 5 km detour.** Use it primarily as a lunch stop unless the group consciously wants a return visit.
- **[Marble Mountains](https://vietnam.travel/things-to-do/around-marble-mountains) — 90–150 min, 1 km detour.** A fallback only if skipped on Day 1 and everyone genuinely wants to stop.

- **Stop priority:** The lake morning and predictable return are fixed; Hội An is lunch, while all other visits are optional.
- **Weather decision:** Rice-field lanes can flood quickly. Use the most direct open surfaced route and protect the arrival time.
- **Arrival plan:** Refuel, return or park the scooters and go home; use an optional Đà Nẵng hotel only when genuinely needed.
